THE CHALLENGE

Automation in software engineering increases coordination costs for teams, slowing down market adaptation. The coming wave of agentic AI demands shared, explicit, domain-specific models to support reliable decision-making. Building the right platform capabilities to manage scope complexity is essential for gaining a joint production advantage.

OUR APPROACH

We’re catching the wave by balancing the relational complexity carried by individuals and building an orchestration engine where cooperative games between teams form effortlessly. This joint interaction context creates the conditions for driving market change. Designed from first principles, Endurance cares about systems — so people can too.

We amplify human intelligence, not replace it.

Things that happen

Events are things that happen once.

We try to understand them from different perspectives, such as user experience or infrastructure.

Our intent is to apply the right action policy — to amplify events that are beneficial and relevant to the experience, and to diminish those that threaten reliability.

> Understanding reliability is everyone's job.

Sense of activity

We care about the conditions in which relevant events happen. They help us form a sense of context and give us the ability to grasp what’s going on.

Traceability — the sense of meaningful activity — is what we aim to preserve over time.

> Causal understanding enable the best possible decisions, under the circumstances, in the midst of change.

Power to run

We build capabilities with the power to run activity the way it needs to be:
   – with all the data required to meet user needs
   – with trusted, correct data available at the right time

Infrastructure calls for data isolation, while domain-specific needs demand deep interconnection to extract value. This tension creates relational complexity that must be balanced through timely, high-quality team decisions.

> Cost of delay is real — in architecture, it’s often hidden in slow decisions, unclear ownership, and trapped knowledge

Flow solves autonomy

We do capability design for Flow — focusing on enabling change in a predictable ways with an affordable rate.

Capability design can be seen as the ‘theory’ or shared model that best explains what is happening. These explicit beliefs support autonomous decision-making and help build more reliable systems.

> Decision quality improves not by centralization, but by designing systems that let the right decisions happen at the right time, with minimal friction.

Engine for Flow

When we don’t design for Flow, shared models become vulnerable to erosion. People responsible for maintaining coherence are often busy and overwhelmed.

Systems fail the way they’re designed. If user experience isn’t embedded from the start, failure is just a matter of time.

Endurance levels up execution by engineering capabilities that keep teams in Flow — adaptive, aligned, and fast.

> Designed for teams’ collective agency — enabling more high-stakes actions with less friction.
